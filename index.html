What is Model UN?

Model United Nations, popularly referred to as MUN, is an academic simulation of the United Nations and its multilateral bodies.

In an MUN participants are assigned roles as diplomats and ambassadors from among the 193 member states of the United Nations. Empowered with a mandate to protect the interests of their populations, they are expected to balance it with the larger good of the world, in sync with the spirit of the United Nations. In this simulated environment, the aim of the participants is to work together in creating a better world through negotiated conflict resolution, drafting solutions to pressing global problems and working on programs and initiatives that offer hope and help to those in need. The MUN format is designed to bring to the fore a dynamic mix of issues that not only affect individual countries but also larger issues concerning the world, presenting an exciting and challenging opportunity to come up with possible and viable solutions. This platform presents each of the participantsâ€™ scope to take on leadership and represent the assigned country in a series of relevant debates and discussions that potentially will stretch their knowledge while exposing them to the intricate challenges of being a Global Leader.

Director General's Address

Dear Prospective Delegates,

Greetings to you from the Illumination United Nations Simulations Secretariat. It is my distinct pleasure to welcome you to participte in the first session of Illumination Model United Nations 2015, to be held in Bhubaneswar from June 27-28, 2015.

With a mission to promote awareness of international issues and use the resources available to us to educate ourselves and our peers, we have promised our delegates to begin a simulation of a different class in itself in the vibrant city of Bhubaneswar and we have given our best to keep up our promise. We hope to deliver a conference that would prove to be educational as well as inspiring at the same time for the participants. Delegates should gain insight into the workings of the United Nations, the dynamics of International Relations regarding the nature of the United Nations, new skills in negotiation, solution-based research, and debate, and above all, new inspiration to pursue further understanding and even change in a range of pressing global issues. ILNMUN presents an unique opportunity for students to meet, discuss, and collaborate with equally motivated and talented peers on a diverse array of topics which will aslo develop their ability to work with their co-delegates on topics of global relevance.

Around 200 delegates from different backgrounds and interests will gather in Bhubaneswar in June 2015 for the first session of Illumination Model United Nations 2015. We look forward to welcoming you and are excited to join you in Bhubaneswar!

Warmest wishes,
Deepraj Pandey
Director-General

Meet the Executive Board

  • General Assembly
  • Security Council

The General Assembly Disarmament and Security Council (GA-DISEC, First Council) is the largest committee at ILNMUN 2015, and it is the place where each of the member states of the UN come together to discuss pressing issues that affect many countries across multiple continents. Debate in the GA is spirited, as delegates must balance their responsibilities to their respective nations, allies, and the committee as a whole. Delegates emerge from a GA committee with a thorough understanding of the promises and pitfalls of international diplomacy.

Chair: Ananya Bharadwaj
Vice-Chair: Siddhant Sarangi


The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) is a committee which is smaller and more intense than other committees. This requires all delegates to respond quickly and decisively to crises, and allows each member of the committee to play a critical role in advancing his or her own interests, fashioning meaningful debate, and crafting peaceful and innovative solutions. This council is supposed to move very quickly due to its relative size and skill level.

Chair: Vaivab Das
Vice-Chair: Shubhjit Behera
